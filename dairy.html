Why It Matters

Milk is one of the most temperature-sensitive commodities. Every 1°C above 4°C accelerates bacterial growth, reducing shelf life and compromising safety. Dairy cooperatives and collection centers face daily challenges with power fluctuations, ambient heat, and high door-opening frequency during milk pouring. Conventional cooling units struggle to maintain consistent temperature, leading to spoilage and financial losses for farmers.
